How to get to CDG Airport from Paris?

RER B, Roissybus, taxi, private car — all transport options to Charles de Gaulle compared. Prices, duration and practical tips.

Back to blog
March 12, 2026 · 7 min read

You have a flight from Charles de Gaulle Airport and you're looking for the best way to get there? Between overcrowded public transport, impossible-to-find taxis and endless shuttles, getting to CDG can quickly become a nightmare. There is, however, a simple, comfortable and stress-free solution.

Why public transport to CDG is an ordeal

You may have been advised to take the RER B or the Roissybus. On paper, it's economical. In reality, here's what awaits you:

The RER B — an obstacle course

  • Packed carriages during rush hour — impossible to sit with luggage
  • Frequent strikes and delays — you stress with every minute lost thinking about your flight
  • Stairs, corridors, connections — with 2 suitcases and a pushchair, it's a nightmare
  • Pickpockets — loaded travellers on the RER B are known targets
  • No service after 00:30 — early morning flight or late arrival? You're stuck

The Roissybus — slow and rigid

  • 60 to 90 minutes depending on traffic — you need to leave very early
  • Single departure point (Opéra) — if you don't live nearby, you first need to get there
  • Fixed timetable — you adapt to the bus, not the other way around
  • No night service

The taxi — a gamble

  • Impossible to find when it rains, during rush hour or on strike days
  • Hidden surcharges — 5th passenger, bulky luggage, the bill climbs
  • Random vehicle quality — sometimes decent, sometimes an unpleasant surprise
  • No flight tracking — your plane is delayed? The taxi won't wait
The reality

You're heading on holiday or a business trip. Your flight is in 3 hours. Do you really want to risk an RER delay, a packed carriage with your suitcases, or an unfindable taxi? Your peace of mind is worth a private chauffeur waiting at your doorstep.
